Welcome to our 30th HKDBF NY 2022
This year we are once again focusing on these 3 major issues: contributing to the reopening of the NYC economy, bringing focus to the anti-Asian + other hate crime issues and promoting vaccinations, and post-covid mental and physical wellness. HKDBFNY 2022 features events for the entire family, an opening day parade at noon on Saturday and the US Dragon Boat Open Championship Race on Sunday. Racing both days from 9am-5pm. Some of the attractions taking place during the festival include several special races. Including the Hong Kong SAR 25th Anniversary Challenge, the Sponsors Challenge, a Media Invitational, the Womens Invitational, and the Seniors Invitational. This year's Corporate Invitational looks to be a good one, with a number of Corporate Teams joining us to race for their companies. There are also special Youth, Educational and Health Care Invitationals and the always exciting and rewarding Charity race, featuring funds raised by the participating teams to be donated to the charities designated by the winning teams. Over the years the Charity Race has contributed in excess of $300,000 to various NYC charities.
The program on the Main Stage showcases the best regional multicultural artists and traditional music/dance from across the globe. We are thrilled to have NY based young rapper Cameron Sean joining us both days to perform his very relevant rap, "Stop the Hate!" The ever popular Shaolin Martial Artists return for performances on both days. We have several other very Special Musical Guests this year, the amazing violinist Daisy Jopling, the Gigs & The Professor Orchestra featuring recording artists Vito Balsamo of Vito and The Salutations, Sandra Taylor vocalist with Barbara Harris and the Toys and George (Tito) Santiago Vocalist with The Eternals. Sunday features Terry Kings of the historic band the DRIFTERS with Charlie Thomas Junior and Benny King's Son Benditto King. The Chinese Theatre Works Co. performs both days with 2 different shows and Master Storyteller Jonathan Kruk returns again, spinning “Dragon Tales.” In addition, we have demonstrations of traditional Asian folk arts and crafts, booths staffed by sponsors and many community-based organizations.
We have several food trucks on site including:
  • Halal Truck
  • Kim's Korean BBQ
  • Cousins Lobster Truck from Maine
Multicultural Radio Broadcasting will be on hand offering a live radio broadcast both days along with reps from Sing Tao, the Queens Courier and Good News Broadcast. Staff from the New York State Of Health will be on site offering Covid shots and testing.
The Hong Kong Economic and Trade Office in New York is proud to continue to support the Hong Kong Dragon Boat Festival in New York as the title sponsor. To mark the 25th anniversary of the establishment of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region, a special mini-exhibition will be staged, featuring works of Hong Kong illustrator Stella So to showcase the intangible cultural heritage of Hong Kong, from celebrated festivals to unique traditions. In addition, visitors are encouraged to join in the games and festivities and win some prizes at our tent, located directly opposite the center stage. Souvenirs will also be handed out to new followers of our Facebook and Instagram “Hong Kong Meets America”. Hong Kong Dragon Riders, the official dragon boat teams of the HKETONY, will compete for championship in various races. The Hong Kong Tourism Board is partnering with Cathay Pacific Airways and Grand Holidays at their booth. Please stop by to enjoy their offerings, meet with the representatives and learn more about Hong Kong.
